This take on the classic Texas Holdem style poker plays more like a casino slot machine. You'll play against the odds of getting the best hands. Gameplay is simple and goes like this:

All poker is the best of 5 cards. Regardless of how many cards are in play, you can only pick 5 cards for your hand. In Texas Holdem, there are 5 cards on the table and two in your hand. Everyone can use the cards on the table, but only you can use the cards in your hand.

  1. When you press play, $5 will be deducted from your wallet and the game will start. Once the game starts, you get your two-card hand. During each round, you can add $5 to your wager. Press Play to move to the next step.
  2. The next step turns the first three cards on the table face up. The turning of these cards is also called the "flop". If you like what you see, you can continue to increase your wager. Press play to move to the next step.
  3. The next step turns the fourth card on the table face up. This is also called the "turn" card or "fourth street." If you like what you see, you can continue to increase your wager. Press play to move to the next step.
  4. The next step turns the fifth card on the table face up. This is also called the "river" card or "fifth street." At this point, the game will determine your best 5 cards for you, and highlight the results. You'll get a payout (or not) based on the hand you got. Press play to setup a new game and keep stacking chips!
Hit "Play" to begin playing. Each game costs $5 in chips to start!
